Pepper Soup

Description

Pepper soup is a light, spicy West African broth made with aromatic spices and meat or fish. It’s known for its warming heat, often enjoyed on rainy days or served at gatherings. The rich, peppery flavor comes from a unique blend of local spices.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg goat meat (or chicken/fish)
  • 2 liters water
  • 2 tablespoons pepper soup spice mix
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust for spice)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 seasoning cubes (or bouillon)
  • 1 teaspoon ground crayfish (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh scent leaves or basil for garnish

Steps

  1. Wash and cut the meat into bite-sized pieces.
  2. Place meat in a pot, season with salt, chopped onions, and seasoning cubes.
  3. Add water and bring to a boil, simmer until meat is tender.
  4. Blend scotch bonnets with a little water, then add to the pot.
  5. Stir in the pepper soup spice mix and crayfish, let it simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  6. Adjust seasoning as needed.
  7. Serve hot, garnished with scent leaves or basil.
